## Executive Summary

Every principled chiropractor has encountered the same clinical puzzle. A patient presents with clear vertebral subluxation. The adjustment is well-delivered. The segment moves. And yet the pattern returns within days — or never fully resolves. Meanwhile, another patient holds their correction for months. What is the difference?

Talsky Tonal Chiropractic (TTC) proposes a mechanism that accounts for this discrepancy: **vertebral subluxation is most accurately understood as a secondary, compensatory event.** The primary interference takes place upstream, in the NeuroSpinal System — the Cranio-Spinal Meningeal Functional Unit — where sustained meningeal contracture alters the system's baseline tone. Muscles move bones. They hold them in compensatory positions as a result of core dural tension, not the other way around. TTC identifies the meningeal system as the **Primary Tone-Setter**: the structure whose baseline tension governs the global patterns that shape posture, adaptability, autonomic regulation, and the persistence of subluxation.

**Why this matters to your practice.** You have likely seen patients whose symptoms resolve without vertebral movement, and patients whose alignment looks clean on film but who cannot hold their health. TTC offers a framework for understanding both phenomena. When the NeuroSpinal System is addressed directly, the vertebral compensation often self-corrects — because the upstream driver has been engaged. TTC does not replace the approaches you already use; it proposes the mechanism that determines whether any approach holds.

This paper extends prior tonal models in a specific direction: Epstein proposed the cord-meningeal system as the initiating site for Class B (facilitated) subluxation. TTC proposes that meningeal involvement is present in all subluxation classes — that even Class A (physically traumatic) subluxation is sustained, in part, through meningeal contracture. The 5 D's cascade (dyskinesia, dysafferentation, dysponesis, dysautonomia, degeneration) has a step upstream: aberrant NeuroSpinal tone.

**TTC is first a model and only second a technique.** The mechanobiological framework presented here — anchored in myofibroblast contractility in meningeal tissue (Dorrier et al., 2021; Tomasek et al., 2002) and established dural-muscular continuities (Scali et al., 2011) — is designed to be evaluated on anatomical plausibility, internal consistency, and testability. Where the model proposes subclinical extensions of established pathological mechanisms, this is stated explicitly using "TTC hypothesis" and "TTC model" framing throughout.
