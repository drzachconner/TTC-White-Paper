### 2. Placement of Pull Quotes, Callouts, Tables, and Diagrams
*   **Pull Quotes & Callout Boxes:** Use these strategically to break up text density and highlight striking statistics or clinical phenomena. Limit them to roughly **one per 2-3 pages** to prevent visual chaos [26, 27]. They should appear frequently in the middle sections (Pages 9-20) to prevent attention decay [26]. Case vignettes should be set off in distinct bordered boxes [28].
*   **Comparison Tables:** Place these in the clinical problem section (Pages 9-12) to help readers organize their observations, and in the clinical protocol section (Pages 17-20) to contrast traditional techniques with reframed tension-first techniques [17, 29].
*   **Diagrams (Anatomical & Graphical):** High-density anatomical diagrams belong explicitly in the middle of the document (Pages 13-16) to provide mechanobiological justification *after* clinical credibility has been established [30].
*   **Chapter-Break Pages:** Insert full or half-page chapter breaks before major sections (roughly every 4-5 pages) with a brief orientation statement to refresh reader attention [31, 32].

### 4. Typography and Color Palette Recommendations
Professional audiences evaluating paradigm-shifting material expect a clean, authoritative "trade journal" aesthetic, not a marketing brochure [36, 37].
*   **Color Palette:** Use **blues and grays** as your primary scheme, as these colors are universally associated with trust, medical authority, and professionalism [37]. Use a dark gray or pure black font on a slightly off-white background to reduce visual strain [38]. Limit yourself to no more than two accent colors (e.g., a muted teal or dark gold) to avoid visual chaos [38-40].
*   **Typography:** 
    *   **Body Text:** Use a clean, modern Sans-serif font (like Helvetica, Lato, or Avenir) at 11–12pt [36, 41]. Keep the text **left-aligned (not full-justified)**, as justification creates uneven word spacing that harms readability [42]. Use a minimum line height of 1.5x [36, 42].
    *   **Headings:** Use a 3-level hierarchy to assist skimmers. H1 (Main Sections) should be 24-36pt bold, H2 (Subsections) 18-24pt bold, and H3 (Tertiary) 12-14pt italicized or in an accent color [36, 43]. You can use an authoritative Serif font (like Garamond or Georgia) for headings to provide elegant contrast against the sans-serif body [36].
*   **White Space:** Ensure at least 1-inch margins on all sides. Use generous spacing between paragraphs (6-12pts) and around images to project quality and reduce cognitive load [37, 38].
