Read the master paper (renditions/01_2026-02-03_MASTER_PAPER_COMPLETE.md) in its entirety. Also read the RESEARCH.md for this phase (.planning/phases/03-credibility-claims/03-RESEARCH.md) which contains the pre-built claim audit inventory, evidence tier definitions, and PEAR/RNG analysis.

Perform a systematic credibility audit organized into five sections:

**1. Three-Tier Evidence Classification System**

Document the three-tier system at the top of the audit file so the author can reference it:
- ESTABLISHED: Systematic reviews, multiple RCTs, standard textbooks, widely replicated. Language: "Research demonstrates," "Evidence shows," state as fact.
- EMERGING: Single RCTs, well-designed observational studies, peer-reviewed mechanistic research with limited clinical validation. Language: "Research suggests," "Evidence indicates," "Studies have found."
- EXPLORATORY: Theoretical frameworks, single small-sample studies, contested meta-analyses, speculative extrapolations. Language: "One line of inquiry...," "Exploratory research has examined...," "This remains a working hypothesis."

Also define two special categories:
- MODEL/THESIS: The paper's own theoretical contribution. Language: "In the TTC model...," "This paper proposes...," "The TTC framework holds that..."
- PHILOSOPHICAL AXIOM: Definitional statements within chiropractic vitalistic tradition. Acceptable as-is for white paper audience.

**2. Section-by-Section Claim Audit**

Go through every section of the paper (Executive Summary, First Principles, Parts I-XII, Glossary). For each section, identify every factual or clinical claim (not metaphors, not rhetorical questions, not technique descriptions). For each claim:
- Quote the claim (first ~25 words for identification)
- Current citation (if any)
- Evidence tier assignment using the three-tier system
- Current language match? YES / PARTIAL / NO
- If NO or PARTIAL: specific issue (e.g., "stated as fact but evidence is EMERGING," "fascial research applied to meninges without bridging language," "no citation provided")
- Recommended rewrite (provide the exact replacement language)

Use the RESEARCH.md claim audit as your primary reference -- it has already cataloged ~30 problem claims. VERIFY each against the current master paper text (Phase 2 may have changed some passages). Also scan for any additional claims the research missed.

Organize by paper section. For sections with no problematic claims (e.g., Parts VII, VIII, X, XII which are model/technique/ethics/metaphor), note "No credibility issues -- section is [model description / technique protocol / etc.]" so the author can see full coverage.

**3. High-Risk Items Analysis**

Create a dedicated subsection for the four highest-risk credibility issues, each with detailed analysis and treatment options:

A. **PEAR/RNG Content (Part IX)** -- The single highest credibility risk.
Present the full evidence landscape from RESEARCH.md:
- PEAR lab history (1979-2007, closed without replicable results)
- Radin & Nelson (1989) meta-analysis and its criticisms
- Bosch, Steinkamp & Boller (2006) Psychological Bulletin meta-analysis findings
- Current scientific consensus

Present three treatment options:
- Option A (RECOMMENDED): Keep in text with heavy exploratory framing. Provide exact rewrite language that acknowledges contested status, failed replications, and methodological criticism. Add Bosch et al. (2006) as balancing citation.
- Option B: Move to appendix with full disclaimers.
- Option C: Remove entirely; let congruent intent stand on neurophysiological basis alone.

B. **Fascial-to-Meningeal Extrapolation** -- Catalog every instance where fascial research (Fede, Langevin, Schleip) is applied to meningeal tissue without acknowledging the inference. Provide bridging language template.

C. **Haavik "20% Prefrontal Cortex" Claim** -- Flag both occurrences (Parts V and IX). Note the actual study (Lelic et al. 2016), its limitations (brain source localization, small sample), and recommend adding the specific citation to References plus hedging the language.

D. **Model-as-Fact Throughout** -- List every sentence where the paper's own thesis/model is stated as established fact. Provide "In the TTC model..." or "This paper proposes..." reframing for each.

**4. Oschman/Becker Citation Quality**

Flag all claims that rely solely on popular science books (Becker & Selden 1985, Oschman 2000) as primary evidence. For each, recommend either replacing with the peer-reviewed primary research those books reference, or adding explicit "As synthesized in [popular account]" framing.

**5. Decision Points for Author**

Compile a numbered list of every decision the author needs to make:
1. PEAR/RNG treatment: Option A (keep with heavy framing), B (appendix), or C (remove)?
2. Haavik "20%" language: approve recommended hedge and addition of Lelic et al. 2016 to References?
3. Model-as-fact framing: approve systematic "In the TTC model..." reframing throughout?
4. Fascial-to-meningeal bridging: approve recommended bridging language template?
5. Oschman/Becker treatment: replace with primary sources, add framing language, or leave as-is?
6. Unsourced historical claims (Part V): approve "According to accounts within the technique community..." framing?
7. Each individual overclaim flag: approve recommended rewrite or keep current language?

Write ALL findings to `.planning/phases/03-credibility-claims/03-01-AUDIT.md` in a clean, scannable format. Use tables where appropriate (matching the RESEARCH.md claim audit table format). Include exact recommended rewrite language for every flagged item so the author can approve or modify specific text.
